Exam Reflection

I chose to reflect on my experience with Exam 3, which focused on more applications of derivatives and the basics of integrals. I started studying about a week before the test because I wanted to give myself enough time to go over everything. I reviewed class notes, past quizzes, and homework assignments to refresh the main concepts. I focused especially on the areas I wasn’t super confident in. Two days before the exam, I went to the Math Lab and got some help with questions I was stuck on, which helped clear up some confusion. Most of my studying was done alone, but I tried to actively work through problems instead of just re-reading my notes. At the time, I really thought I was preparing the right way. I felt like I had a decent understanding, so I didn’t expect the integrals to completely throw me off—but they did. When I got to those problems, it was like my brain shut down, and I didn’t know how to approach them at all.
I got my exam grade and saw that I failed, I felt really disappointed in myself. I didn’t expect to do that badly, and it made me realize that I needed to study more deeply and give myself more time with the harder concepts—especially integrals. I looked back through the test to figure out where I went wrong and noticed that the types of problems I avoided or didn’t practice enough were the ones I struggled with the most. I also talked to a tutor to go over my mistakes and make sure I understood the correct steps. This helped a lot and gave me a clearer idea of what to focus on next time. I want to take this as a learning experience and not let it discourage me. For the next exam, I plan to start even earlier, do more practice with tricky problems, and test myself more often so I feel more confident and prepared.
